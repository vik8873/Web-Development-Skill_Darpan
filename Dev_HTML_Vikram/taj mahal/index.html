History of the Taj Mahal

Published on November 13, 2024

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the world's most iconic monuments, built as a symbol of love and remembrance. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. Construction of this majestic mausoleum took approximately 22 years to complete, involving over 20,000 artisans and craftsmen. The primary architect was believed to be Ustad Ahmad Lahori, who integrated Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural styles into the design. The Taj Mahal's central structure, made of pristine white marble, is complemented by its symmetrical gardens, reflecting pools, and red sandstone gateways. The Taj Mahal houses the tombs of both Mumtaz Mahal and Shah Jahan, symbolizing eternal love. Its intricate inlay work with semi-precious stones, verses from the Quran, and the play of light on its marble surface make it a marvel of craftsmanship. In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is celebrated as a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage, attracting millions of visitors annually.

Planning Your Visit

Published on November 11, 2024

Visitors can explore the Taj Mahal throughout the year, with sunrise and sunset offering the most breathtaking views. Agra is easily accessible by train or road from Delhi. Ensure you get your tickets in advance to enjoy a seamless visit.

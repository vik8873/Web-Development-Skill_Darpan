Main Concepts of Flexbox

  1. Flex Container: The parent element that holds flex items. Apply display: flex; or display: inline-flex; to the container.
  2. Flex Items: The child elements inside the flex container.

Flex Container Properties

These properties are applied to the flex container and influence its behavior and the layout of its child items.

1. Display

Defines the container as a flex container.

  • Values:
    • flex: Creates a block-level flex container.
    • inline-flex: Creates an inline-level flex container.

2. Flex-direction

Specifies the direction of the main axis and determines how items are placed in the container.

  • Values:
    • row (default): Items are placed left to right.
    • row-reverse:Items are placed right to left.
    • column:Items are placed top to bottom.
    • column-reverse:Items are placed bottom to top.

3. Justify-content

Aligns items along the main axis (horizontal by default).

  • Values:
    • flex-start (default):Items align to the start.
    • FLex-end:Items align to the end.
    • Centre:Items align to the center to main axis.
    • space-between: Equal space between items.
    • space-around:Equal space around items.
    • space-evenly:Equal space distributed between and around items.

4. Align-items

Aligns items along the cross axis (vertical by default).

  • Values:
    • stretch (default):Items stretch to fill the container.
    • flex-start:Align items at the start.
    • flex-end:Align items at the end.
    • center:Align items at the center.
    • baseline:Align items along their baseline.

5. Align-content

Aligns multiple lines of items (if wrapping is enabled) along the cross axis.

  • Values:
    • stretch (default):Lines stretch to fill space.
    • flex-start:Lines align at the start.
    • flex-end:Lines align at the end.
    • center:Lines align at the center.
    • space-between:Equal space between lines.
    • space-around:Equal space around lines.

6. Flex Wrap

Controls whether items wrap to the next line.

  • Values:
    • nowrap (default):All items stay on a single line.
    • wrap:Items wrap to the next line.
    • wrap-reverse:Items wrap to the next line in reverse order.
